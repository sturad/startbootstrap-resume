Work Values

  • I care about users. I've been a technology trainer and customer support person and can bring patience and culture awareness to cross-team and user interactions. I love it when business value and customer value are in consensus.
  • Listen to internal users. These are usually the power users who are drinking the champagne of your software product. If they think something will be a problem operationally, it quite probably will. Of course, you can not be wild-eyed reactive to any feedback, but you should include all feedback in the calculus of whether your product is suceeding and how it needs to change.
  • Lifes better with testing. I value Test Driven and Behavior Driven Development, but also exploratory programming and prototyping. Once you are warmed-up on how you might proceed with technology, you will be better off if you are prepared to throw away some or all of the prototype, write tests, and rewrite code. Code that is testable is easier to work with further so the sooner you can get back on TDD if you jump off to prototype, the better off you are.
  • I can interface with the entire team and process. I've worked closed with or been part of Project Management or Product Design teams. Filling out JIRA tickets doesn't make me depressed or waste meetings with nerd rage! I'm also fine functioning in low process/ceremony teams.
  • A balance between innovation and realism keeps the team and the business happy and on the same page. I think theory matters and enjoy talking with technically idealistic team members. I do understand that organizations need pragamtic solutions and to be strategic in their innovation.
  • I know about the Broken Window theory and technology debt. I enjoy watching artisans at work and absorbing some of their successful behaviors. One thing I observed with the strongest developers is that they know that with houses and software, if one window is broken and doesn't get fixed, the neighborhood will deteriorate more quicky. Teams prosper when they agree on professional disciplines (such as TDD) and the product and morale can erode when you break disciplines. However, You can't usually pay off all technology debt immediately. The happiest teams work with product and project management to make sure time gets alloted sooner rather than later to address software entropy.
  • I take delivery seriously When I'm engaged to work for an organization, I do my best to help delivery happen. I know the man month is mythical, but still I will put the time in effort to help bring success.
